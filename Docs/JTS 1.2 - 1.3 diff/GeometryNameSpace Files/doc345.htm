37: /**
38:  *  A simple, planar <code>Surface</code> bounded by one exterior <code>LinearRing</code>
39:  *  (the "shell") and zero or more interior <code>LinearRing</code>s (the
40:  *  "holes"). <P>
41:  *
42:  *  The shell and holes of a <code>Polygon</code> are <code>LinearRing</code>s.
43:  *  The SFS definition of <code>Polygon</code> has the following implications:
44:  *
45:  *  <UL>
46:  *    <LI> The shell and holes cannot self-intersect
47:  *    <LI> Holes can touch the shell or another hole at a single point only.
48:  *    This means that holes cannot intersect one another at multiple points or
49:  *    in a line segment.
50:  *    <LI> Polygon interiors must be connected
51:  *    <LI> There is no requirement that a point where a hole touches the shell
52:  *    be a vertex.
53:  *  </UL>
54:  *  Note that the SFS definition of <code>Polygon</code> differs from that in
55:  *  some other commonly used spatial models. For instance, the ESRI ArcSDE
56:  *  spatial model allows shells to self-intersect at vertices, but does not
57:  *  allow holes to touch the shell. The SFS and the ArcSDE model are equivalent
58:  *  in the sense that they allow describing exactly the same set of areas.
59:  *  However, they may require different polygon structures to describe the same
60:  *  area. <P>
61:  *
62:  *  <code>Polygon</code>s do not have cut lines, spikes or punctures. <P>
63:  *
64:  *  Two boundary rings may intersect at one point at most. <P>
65:  *
66:  *  Empty <code>Polygons</code> may not contain holes. <P>
67:  *
68:  *  Since the shell and holes of <code>Polygons</code> are <code>LinearRing</code>
69:  *  s, there is no requirement on their orientation. They may be oriented either
70:  *  clockwise or counterclockwise. <P>
71:  *
72:  *  For a precise definition of a polygon, see the <A
73:  *  HREF="http://www.opengis.org/techno/specs.htm">OpenGIS Simple Features
74:  *  Specification for SQL</A> .
